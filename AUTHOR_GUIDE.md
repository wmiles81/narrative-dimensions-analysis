## What This Tool Does (In Plain English)

Think of your story as a journey through emotional space. This framework helps you:

- **Plan** character arcs that feel earned and satisfying
- **Diagnose** why a scene feels flat or rushed
- **Validate** that your story matches genre expectations
- **Fix** pacing problems before beta readers see them

**No coding required.** You just have conversations with Claude.

## The Five Dimensions You Need to Know

You don't need to track all the technical stuff. Focus on these five:

### 1. **Intimacy** (0-10)
How emotionally close are the characters?
- 0 = Strangers
- 5 = Friends
- 10 = Soul-deep connection

### 2. **Trust** (0-10)
Do they believe in each other?
- 0 = Complete distrust
- 5 = Cautious
- 10 = Total faith

### 3. **Stakes** (0-10)
How much is at risk?
- 0 = Nothing matters
- 5 = Moderate consequences
- 10 = Everything on the line

### 4. **Desire/Goal Alignment** (0-10)
Do they want the same thing?
- 0 = Complete opposition
- 5 = Partial overlap
- 10 = Perfect alignment

### 5. **Vulnerability** (0-10)
Are their emotional walls up or down?
- 0 = Completely guarded
- 5 = Cautiously open
- 10 = Fully exposed

**That's it!** Track these five and Claude can calculate everything else.

### "Why Does My Romance Feel Flat?"

**You say:**
```
My romance feels flat around chapter 10. Current state:

Intimacy: 7
Trust: 7
Desire: 8
Vulnerability: 7
Stakes: 3

They're getting along fine but there's no tension. What's wrong?
```

**Claude might say:**
```
The problem is everything is too harmonious. Romance needs gaps:

- High desire (8) but low proximity = yearning
- High vulnerability (7) but low trust = risk
- Or misaligned goals = conflict

Your stakes are also too low (3). Try:
- Stakes: 7 (raise external pressure)
- Trust: 4 (introduce a secret or misunderstanding)
- Vulnerability: 8 (keep this high so betrayal hurts)

This creates the "vulnerability-trust gap" that generates romantic tension.
```

### "Is This Too Intense for Cozy Fantasy?"

**You say:**
```
I'm worried my cozy fantasy is too dark. My "dark night" has:

- Protagonist completely breaks down
- Stakes at 10 (life or death)
- Trust crashes from 7 to 0
- Feels like everything is lost

Is this too much for cozy?
```

**Claude might say:**
```
Yes, that's thriller physics, not cozy. For cozy fantasy:

Maximum darkness should be:
- Stakes: 6-7 (personal, not life/death)
- Trust: drops to 3-4 (strained, not shattered)
- Character feels "inadequate" not "destroyed"

Cozy dark night = "I can't do this" + cup of tea
Thriller dark night = "Everyone will die" + no hope

Soften yours by:
- Lower the stakes (relationships at risk, not lives)
- Keep community support visible
- Have the "comfort" element present even in crisis
```

### "How Do I Fix Pacing?"

**You say:**
```
Chapters 8-12 drag. Nothing seems to happen. How do I fix pacing
using this framework?
```

**Claude might say:**
```
Pacing = how fast dimensions change. If chapters drag, dimensions
are stuck.

Check: What changed from Chapter 8 ‚Üí 12?
- Intimacy: 5 ‚Üí 5 (stuck!)
- Trust: 6 ‚Üí 6 (stuck!)
- Stakes: 4 ‚Üí 4 (stuck!)

**Fix: Move 2-3 dimensions per chapter**

Chapter 8 ‚Üí 9:
- Intimacy: 5 ‚Üí 6 (shared vulnerable moment)
- Stakes: 4 ‚Üí 6 (external complication)

Chapter 9 ‚Üí 10:
- Trust: 6 ‚Üí 5 (small misunderstanding)
- Desire: 7 ‚Üí 8 (proximity increases)

Chapter 10 ‚Üí 11:
- Intimacy: 6 ‚Üí 7 (confession)
- Trust: 5 ‚Üí 7 (misunderstanding cleared)

Now it feels like progress!
```

## Genre Cheat Sheet

### **Cozy Fantasy**
- Gentle emotional changes (no whiplash)
- Stakes stay personal (not world-ending)
- Must end happy and whole
- Dark night around 70-75%

### **Dark Romance**
- Intense emotional swings allowed
- Power dynamics must shift
- High stakes throughout
- Dark night very late (80%+)

### **Thriller**
- Rapid changes create unease
- Trust constantly tested
- High stakes sustained
- Can end with wounds

### **Romance (any subgenre)**
- Must cross from "wounded" to "whole"
- Relationship must close (distant ‚Üí intimate)
- Black moment required (around 75%)
- Must end together

### **Mystery**
- Information reveals paced steadily
- Community trust builds
- Stakes moderate (puzzle > danger)
- Must solve mystery

## FAQs

### "Do I need to track numbers for every chapter?"
**No!** Just at key moments:
- Opening
- End of Act 1
- Midpoint
- Dark night
- Climax
- Ending

### "What if I don't know the exact numbers?"
**That's fine!** Just describe it:
- "They're getting closer but still cautious" = Intimacy ~5-6, Trust ~5
- "High stakes, everything matters" = Stakes 8-9
- "Total strangers" = Intimacy 0-1

Claude can translate descriptions to numbers.

### "Do I have to use all the dimensions?"
**No!** Start with the five basics:
1. Intimacy
2. Trust
3. Stakes
4. Goal alignment
5. Vulnerability

Add others (danger, mystery, power differential) only if relevant to your genre.

### "What if my story doesn't fit a single genre?"
**Tell Claude you're blending!**
```
I'm writing a romantic thriller (60% thriller, 40% romance).
What physics constraints apply?
```

### "Can I ignore the 'physics' stuff and just get advice?"
**Absolutely!** You can ask in plain English:
```
My protagonist feels stuck. How do I make her grow?
```

Claude will apply the framework behind the scenes.
